This study examines how people combine information in their attempt to decide whether products are profitable or not. You will be shown information cards, each representing a single case. On each card you will see an amount of investment a person made, and the outcome that resulted from that investment.

Your task is to compare investments and their outcomes in order to consider whether the investments are profitable or not. After presenting 36 cards, we shall ask for your assessment of the profitability of investing in the product in question.

All in all, you will be shown a total of 72 cases, in two lists of 36 pairs, with each list showing the results for one of the two stocks. After the second list we shall also ask which stock you would prefer to invest in using some extra money we shall give you for that decision.

Please pay close attention to each of the cases. From time to time, we shall ask you whether the amount invested or the amount obtained in the most recent transaction exceeds a certain value. If you answer this question incorrectly three times, we shall void your participation in the study.

The task will take about 15 minutes, and you will be paid 2.50 GBP plus a small bonus for your participation.

Devices you can use to take this study:

  • Desktop
  • Mobile
  • Tablet

It is important to pay close attention to the values shown on each card. Occasionally, the presentation will pause, and you will be asked about the card you have just seen. If you answer incorrectly twice, your submission will be voided.
